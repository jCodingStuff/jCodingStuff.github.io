Research

During the second year of my Bachelor's, I had the opportunity to join the Honours+ Programme at UM. An interdisciplinary research group was formed by students of Data Science, European Law, Dutch Law, European Studies and Economics to tackle the study "Young voters of EU". Supervised by Dr. Eliyahu V. Sapir, Assistant Professor in Social Science Research Methods, we aimed to understand the disconnection between the youth and European politics and to offerplausible solutions. Our team ended up winning the Honours+ Challenge and was awarded a grant to continue the research. However, given the circumnstances (graduation, study abroad, etc.), that was the end for us.

For my Bachelor Thesis, I collaborated with APG to perform research on stochastic optimization. I extended a clever approach based on Lagrangian Duality (LD) to optimize the agent roster of their call center. The LD approach was compared to one of the lastest population-based optimization methods: Elephant Herding Optimization (EHO). LD showcased solutions of similar quality as EHO but with lower variance, in exchange for a significantly longer execution time. The paper is available here.
